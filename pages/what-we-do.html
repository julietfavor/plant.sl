What We Do

Making a Real Difference Through Faith and Action

Our Four Pillars of Impact

Plant Network Sierra Leone works across four core areas to advance the Great Commission and transform communities. Each pillar is integrated and holistic, addressing the spiritual, educational, economic, and humanitarian needs of the people we serve.

📢 Preach: Evangelism & Discipleship

Our primary mission is to share the Gospel of Jesus Christ and establish new faith communities across Sierra Leone.

⛪

Church Planting

We identify communities with little Christian presence and plant new churches that become centers of spiritual growth and social transformation.

📖

Street Outreach

Our teams conduct evangelistic outreaches in towns, neighborhoods, and markets, sharing the Gospel and inviting people to follow Christ.

👥

Bible Studies

We organize group Bible studies and discipleship programs to help new believers grow in their faith and understanding of Scripture.

Impact 2024: Planted 3 new churches • Reached 500+ people through outreach • Discipled 150+ new believers

📚 Teach: Educational Support

Education is a path out of poverty. We provide scholarships, learning materials, and teacher training to empower the next generation of Sierra Leoneans.

🎓

Student Scholarships

We sponsor school fees for bright, deserving students from low-income families, removing financial barriers to education.

📚

Learning Materials

We provide books, notebooks, pens, and other educational materials to students and schools in underserved communities.

🏫

Teacher Training

We partner with schools to provide professional development for teachers, improving educational quality and student outcomes.

Impact 2024: Sponsored 120+ students • Distributed 5,000+ learning materials • Trained 40+ teachers

💼 Empower: Economic Empowerment

We believe in sustainable development. Through farming cooperatives, savings groups, and vocational training, we help families achieve economic independence.

🌾

Farming Cooperatives

We establish cooperative farms where families pool resources, share knowledge, and increase agricultural production for household food security and income.

💰

Savings Groups

We organize village savings groups that help families save money, access small loans, and build financial resilience.

🔧

Vocational Training

We train youth and adults in practical skills like tailoring, carpentry, welding, and other trades that create employment opportunities.

Impact 2024: Established 8 farming cooperatives • Formed 15 savings groups • Trained 200+ youth in vocational skills

🤲 Serve: Humanitarian Relief

We respond to urgent community needs through food distribution, clean water access, and medical outreach, demonstrating Christ's love in practical ways.

🍚

Food Distribution

During hunger seasons and emergencies, we distribute rice, beans, and other essentials to vulnerable families.

💧

Clean Water Access

We drill wells and install water pumps in communities lacking access to safe drinking water, preventing waterborne diseases.

⚕️

Medical Outreach

We organize health clinics providing basic medical care, health education, and disease prevention services.

Impact 2024: Distributed food to 1,000+ families • Provided access to clean water for 2,000+ people • Held 10 medical clinics reaching 500+ patients

How We Work

Our Integrated Approach

We don't see these four pillars as separate programs. Instead, they work together to address the whole person—spirit, mind, body, and economic wellbeing. A family that receives a scholarship also benefits from spiritual discipleship. A farmer in our cooperative also hears the Gospel and receives medical care. This holistic approach creates lasting transformation.

Community Partnership

We don't impose solutions from outside. We listen to communities, understand their real needs, and work alongside local leaders to develop sustainable solutions they own and maintain.

Sustainability Focus

We build programs that communities can sustain long-term. Our goal is not dependency on outside aid, but empowerment and independence. Every project is designed with an exit strategy.

Spiritual Foundation

Above all, we minister in the name of Jesus Christ. Every program is an opportunity to share the Gospel and invite people into relationship with God. Material help without spiritual hope is incomplete.
